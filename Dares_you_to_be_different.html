Objectives of the Activity

  • To create awareness about disability and inclusion through multiple perspectives.
  • To encourage dialogue on representation, accessibility, and dignity for differently abled individuals.
  • To highlight the role of media, educational institutions, and individuals in shaping inclusive attitudes.
  • To provide a platform for lived experiences that challenge stereotypes and promote empathy.

Synopsis of the Activity

The panel discussion brought together voices that do not often share the same stage—journalism, academia, social work, and student experience—each offering distinct yet interconnected perspectives on disability and inclusion. The session opened with contextual remarks that introduced the theme "Not Less, Different", emphasizing difference as diversity rather than deficit.

Mrs. Priti Salian, a journalist, spoke on the influential role of media in shaping public perception. She highlighted how disability is often portrayed through narratives of pity or inspiration, rather than authenticity. She emphasized responsible storytelling as a powerful tool to build empathy, awareness, and dignity.

Dr. Abilash Chandran offered an academic perspective on inclusion within higher education. He stressed that inclusivity must move beyond infrastructure and symbolic gestures, and be embedded in institutional policies, teaching practices, and campus culture. His reflections brought attention to the often-unseen challenges faced by differently abled students in academic spaces.

Mr. Abishek Chetty, a student panelist, shared his lived experiences of navigating university life as a differently abled individual. Speaking with honesty and confidence, he reflected on agency, acceptance, and resilience within systems not originally designed for inclusivity. His contribution added authenticity and emotional depth to the discussion.

The session concluded as a reflective call to action, encouraging participants to reconsider attitudes, language, and everyday practices that shape inclusion within the university and beyond.

Highlights of the Activity

  • Diverse panel representing media, academia, social work, and student experience
  • Honest discussions on disability, representation, and accessibility
  • Strong engagement from participants through reflective listening
  • Emphasis on lived experiences as a source of learning
  • Creation of a thoughtful and inclusive dialogue space

Key Takeaways

The panel discussion reinforced that disability is not a limitation but a difference that deserves recognition with dignity and respect. A major takeaway was the importance of inclusive and empowering language, moving away from labels that marginalize. The discussion highlighted that accessibility goes beyond physical infrastructure and includes attitude, awareness, and agency.

The role of media in shaping public narratives was emphasized as crucial in transforming perceptions—from sympathy-driven portrayals to stories of autonomy and value. Institutional responsibility emerged as another key theme, with a strong call for universities to create learning environments that are inclusive both structurally and socially. Overall, the session inspired participants to become active allies in fostering inclusion.

Summary of the Activity

The panel discussion "Not Less, Different" organized by the Centre for Peace Praxis at CHRIST (Deemed to be University) on 25 July 2025 focused on disability, inclusion, and representation. Featuring perspectives from journalism, academia, social work, and student experience, the session encouraged participants to rethink accessibility, language, and empathy in everyday life. The event successfully fostered awareness, reflection, and a shared commitment to inclusive community building.
